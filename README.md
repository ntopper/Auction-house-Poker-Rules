# Auction House Poker — Rules

Auction House Poker is a poker variant where players compete in a series of open auctions to improve their hands. Each player starts with two private cards, and one card at a time is revealed and auctioned to the table. Players may win at most one auction, paying chips into the pot to claim a visible card while others must decide whether to spend or conserve resources.

After all auctions are complete, players reveal their hands before the community cards are dealt, creating full information before the final outcome. The best five-card poker hand wins, with the pot made entirely from antes and auction bids. The game emphasizes valuation, timing, and reading opponents rather than traditional betting.

## 1. Setup
- Standard 52-card deck.  
- Each hand, all players must pay a mandatory **ante (table minimum / blind-equivalent)** to participate.  
- The pot consists of **antes and auction bids only**.  
- Each player is dealt **2 face-down cards**.  
- Dealer button rotates **clockwise** each hand.

---

## 2. Auction Phase
- The game includes **one auction round for each player at the table**.  
- In each round:
  1. Dealer **burns 1 card face down**.  
  2. Dealer reveals **1 face-up auction card** (remains face up).  
  3. Bidding begins with the **dealer** and proceeds **clockwise**.

### Bidding
- Actions: **pass, bid, raise**.  
- The **minimum opening bid** equals the ante amount, even if uncontested.  
- Raises follow standard minimum raise rules.

- Bidding ends when:
  - One player remains, or  
  - All players pass, or  
  - No player can continue bidding.

### Resolution
- Winning bidder:
  - Takes the auction card (remains face up).  
  - Adds their bid to the pot.  
- If all players pass, the card remains face up and unclaimed.

### Restriction
- Each player may win **at most one auction card per hand**.  
- After winning, they may not bid again.

---

## 3. Showdown
- **After the final auction round**, all players proceed to showdown, including players who did not win any auction.

1. All players **reveal their private cards** (including any won auction card).  
2. Dealer reveals **4 community cards**.  
3. Players form the best **5-card hand** using their cards (2–3) + community cards.  
4. Standard poker rankings apply.

---

## 4. Winning
- Best hand wins the pot.  
- Ties split evenly.

---

## 5. Notes
- No betting rounds beyond auctions.  
- All auction cards remain face up and visible.  
- Burn cards remain hidden.  
- Players unable to meet a bid must pass.
